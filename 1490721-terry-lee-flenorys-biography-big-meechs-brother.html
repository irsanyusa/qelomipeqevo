Who is Terry Lee Flenory?

Terry Lee Flenory, alias Southwest T, is an American businessman and music executive. He is the co-founder of Black Mafia Family (BMF) and BMF Entertainment. The latter is a music label, while BMF is an organisation known for illegal activities.

Where does Terry Flenory come from?

The record label co-founder was born in Detroit, Michigan, United States of America. His nationality is American, and his ethnicity is African-American.

Who are Terry Lee Flenory’s parents?

Terry Lee Flenory’s father is Charles, while his mother is Lucille. He was raised alongside two siblings: a brother, Demetrius Edward Flenory Sr., and a sister, Nicole.

Why is Terry Lee Flenory famous?

Southwest T and his brother Big Meech are well known for money laundering and trafficking illegal substances across the United States of America.

The Flenory brothers started trafficking substances while in high school. The two boys were initially known as the 50 Boyz.

They co-founded Black Mafia Family (BMF), a Detroit-based substance trafficking and money laundering organisation, in 1985.

The Flenory brothers expanded their illegal trade networks to other states. By 2000, the brothers had established a large organisation overseeing multi-kilogram distribution sales of illegal and addictive substances in multiple American states. At its peak, BMF had over 500 employees.

The brothers also own a music label called BMF Entertainment. They started the music business in the early 2000s and used it as a front organisation to launder money from the sale of illegal substances and to legitimise their business.

BMF Entertainment was also established to promote the growth of hip-hop music. It was a legit talent promotion agency that handled many high-profile artists, including Trina, Diddy, Jay-Z, T.I., Young Jeezy, and Fabolous.

Did Big Meech and Terry fall out?

In 2003, the Flenory brothers feuded and went separate ways. Southwest T relocated to Los Angeles to head his organisation, while Big Meech remained in Atlanta.

During this period, Big Meech expressed worry that his brother's partying and flamboyant lifestyle would unearth their illegal operations.

Eventually, the brothers were arrested and pleaded guilty to running a criminal enterprise. In 2008, Southwest T and Big Meech were sentenced to 30 years in prison each.
