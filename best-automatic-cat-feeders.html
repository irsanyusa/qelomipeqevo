Best Automatic Cat Feeders: Which is Right for Your Pet?

Yes, your cat loves you. But never forget that underneath that cute, furry exterior beats the heart of a ferocious jungle killer. That ravenous beast needs feeding, and it’s not always going to be on your ideal schedule, unless you particularly love ripping open bags of cat food at three in the morning. If your busy day overlaps with your cat’s regularly scheduled feeding time, an automatic cat feeder is the only sensible option. We’ve looked at what’s available and rounded up our picks for the eight best automatic cat feeders on the meowket.

What to consider when buying an automatic cat feeder

Is there a battery in case my home loses power while I’m away?

A vital feature to consider include a battery backup to ensure your cat will have access to food even if your home loses power.

Do I need Wi-Fi for my automatic cat feeder?

Wi-Fi smart functionality is important so you can manage portion control and feeding times from your smart device.

How big should my automatic cat feeder be?

Your automatic cat feeder’s capacity should be large enough to hold enough meals to keep your cat fed throughout the day (or night). Don’t go too big, though, with the thought that a larger capacity would mean fewer refills. Cat food left in a feeder longer than the manufacturer’s recommended time period could grow mold.

Can automatic cat feeders offer wet food?

It’s best to choose a model capable of dispensing your cat’s favorite dry kibble or wet food. If you plan to be away for long, it is important to know that most wet cat food should always be covered and refrigerated. Your automatic feeder should be able to do both.

What’s the retailer’s return policy?

Cats can have their own preferences when it comes to, well, everything. You may find out that your cat avoids using the automatic feeder, or does not like a specific noise it makes.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

How do I train my cat to use an automatic pet feeder?

Massive spoiler alert: Some cats do not react well to change. That said, there are a few ways to convince a suspicious moggy to switch over to their new automatic pet feeder. Setting it up beside the cat’s old food bowl is a good start, making sure to keep it there until they’re used to eating from it. Filling it with a single meal at a time will reinforce the fact that this is where your cat should expect to be fed from now on, and sprinkling a few tasty treats into your cat’s regular food certainly won’t hurt when it comes to convincing them to give it a try.

What kind of food can I put in an automatic feeder?

The vast majority of automatic cat feeders are made to dispense dry food from a storage compartment that keeps kibble from going stale. Since wet cat food will go bad after sitting out for a few hours, and feeders capable of cooling and storing wet food in an airtight seal are rare, in general, you should expect to fill your automatic feeder with your cat’s preferred dry food.

Can I leave my cat alone with an automatic feeder?

You can certainly leave your cat alone with the automatic feeder while you’re at work or on a day trip without any issues. However, if you’re going to be away for a long time, try to make plans for someone to take care of your cat. They may be finicky at times, but your cat will get lonely on its own after a while, so consider your automatic cat feeder as a helpful supplementary tool, not a cat sitter.

What’s the difference between smart automatic feeders and. “dumb” automatic feeders?

Both traditional “dumb” gravity feeders and high-tech smart cat feeders have their pros and cons. Gravity feeders are generally more affordable, do not rely on AC power or batteries to function, and can be set inside or outside as needed. On the other hand, the simple gravity-powered design of these feeders allows particularly greedy cats to continue to eat food until it’s all gone.

Smart automatic cat feeders tend to be pricier and are generally made for indoor use only, but they’re designed with features that allow cat owners to feed their cat on a specific schedule, with perfect portion control for each serving. Some models utilize a convenient smartphone app, so pet owners can make feeding adjustments on the go and receive notifications when its food compartment runs low, or even a camera and microphone combination that allows you to check in with your cat at any time.
